About Me

From building frameworks to building businesses

I started out creating open-source tools like LIPS.js, iframe.io, webview.io, Kenx, and Cubic Studio because I wanted to make development easier for everyone. That same drive led me to start building companies that solve real problems.

These days, I'm focused on mobility and supply chain tech. With De., I'm building a platform that makes logistics actually work the way it should. Through HealMatrix, I'm tackling the fragmented healthcare infrastructure that affects millions across Africa. And Mijo Ride is coming soon to make getting around easier for everyone.

I'm based in Accra, Ghana, and I still love contributing to open source when I can. It keeps me grounded and connected to the developer community that helped me get here.

Experiments & Pivots

2023 - 2025

Roll Mobility

E-scooter sharing service

Launched an e-scooter sharing service for university campuses — the app, the hardware, the whole thing.

What happened: The university partnerships with KNUST and Legon didn't hold, and without institutional buy-in, the model couldn't move forward.
Lesson: A good solution in the wrong environment is just an expensive prototype.
2023 - 2024

Modela

Draw your app & deploy it

I was tired of the Figma-to-code gap and no-code tools that give you templates but not real control.

What happened: Modela was meant to collapse design and development into one process — draw it, configure it, ship it
Lesson: Some ideas are right but early. Timing and resources have to meet the ambition. Still in active development.
2022 - 2023

Cubic Studio

Web-base IDE & sandbox

A browser-based IDE and sandbox for building remotely-embeddable web apps. It had a clear use case tied to an LMS project that was its primary market.

What happened: When that LMS project pivoted, the studio lost its anchor and got shelved.
Lesson: Building on top of someone else's roadmap is a risk. Validate your own market before you build.
2021 - 2024

Wigo

Payment System

An e-wallet and payment portal built on a Mobile Money model. We built the full system, launched the app, and ran integrations with telecom providers.

What happened: The fintech compliance maze hit — licenses, regulations, and capital requirements we weren't positioned to absorb.
Lesson: Without substantial backing and deep regulatory networks, fintech will drain you before you gain traction.
2017 - 2018

Xyclone OS

Web-based operating system

A customizable web-based OS not tied to any single device — accessible from anywhere, configurable by the user.

What happened: Got the interface and core features built, but hitting native OS capabilities without WebAssembly (which wasn't viable yet) was a hard ceiling.
Lesson: Big-stack projects need proportional resources. This one gave me more than it cost me in learning.

I've spent most of this journey walking a bridge I couldn't see. Not because I was certain it was there — but because stopping wasn't an option. More than once, the safety rope was already gone. Turns out, it was never what was moving me forward.
